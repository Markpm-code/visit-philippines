Tourist Attractions

Banaue Rice Terraces

The Banaue Rice terraces were carved into the mountains of Banaue Ifugao, in the Philippines, by the igorot people.

This Banaue rice terraces are among the Philippines top tourist destinations. It is also believed that thesse terraces were created and were carved mostly by hands.

Chocolate Hills

The majestic Chocolate Hills is one of the Philippines' most popular tourist attractions and is the top destination for Bohol tours. It got its name from its strange yet unique formation and rich vegetation that turns into chocolate brown during the dry months.

The Chocolate Hills are a geological formation in the Bohol province of the Philippines. There are at least 1,260 hills but there may be as many as 1,776 hills spread over an area of more than 50 square kilometers. They are covered in green grass that turns brown during the dry season.

Manila Ocean Park

The Manila Ocean Park is a oceanarium in Manila, Philippines. It is the first world class marine theme park in the philippines

It is a very popular attractions for families locally and tourist. It houses 14000 sea creatures from 277 species.

Mayon Volcano

The Mayon Volcano in the Albay province on Luzon Island is a must-see in the Philippines. It is an active stratovolcano with an elevation of more than 2,400 meters. The Mayon volcano has a perfect cone peak.

Popular with climbers and clampers, It is called the world's most perfect volcanic cone because of its symmetric shape.

El Nido, Palawan

El Nido is a Philippine municipality on Palawan island. It,s known for white-sand beaches, coral reefs and as the gateway to the Bacuit archipelago, a group of islands with steep karst cliffs.

El Nido is known as the Philippines' last frontier, and if you're looking for magnificent beaches in an unspoiled setting, it's a perfect place for you to holiday.

Underground River, Palawan

The Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park is a protected area in the Philippines. The park is located in the Saint Paul Mountain Range on the western coast of the island of Palawan, about 80 kilometers north of the city of Puerto Princesa, and contains the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River

Puerto-Princesa Subterranean River National Park encompasses one of the world's most impressive cave systems, featuring spectacular limestone karst landscapes, pristine natural beauty, intact old-growth forests, and distinctive wildlife. It is located in the south-western part of the Philippine Archipelago on the mid-western coast of Palawan, approximately 76 km northwest of Puerto Princesa and 360 km southwest of Manila.

Boracay

Boracay is a small island in the central Philippines. It's known for its resorts and beaches. White Beach is backed by palm trees, bars, and restaurants along the west coast. Offshore, coral reefs and shipwrecks are home to diverse marine life.

Boracay is famous for its pristine white beaches, thrilling water activities, and great nightlife experiences. While it's popular for the beaches, there are other more places to visit in Boracay that will not disappoint.

Sugba Lagoon, Siargao

Sugba Lagoon is one of Siargao's top rated destinations. It's resembles a strange maze of vibrant water separated by small islands.

Sugba Lagoon is located in Del Carmen, Surigao del Norte and it is one of the tourist attractions in the Philippines.
